About

Hi! I'm Dave Santore. Personally, I’m a lifelong experimenter and “tinkerer” of…anything! Professionally, my goal has always been to find a true solution vs. a surface level fix for my clients. Combining my professional goal with my core personality, I immediately found myself drawn towards the technical side of client projects and would always research ways to build their solutions. Engineering became a component of my role, but I felt it was more than a job: it was a passion to run with.

I enjoy learning and applying newfound skills to my work and my approach to software engineering has been no different, such as deconstructing problems, Agile methodologies, and adhering to strict version control - disciplines I intend to bring to your team. I appreciate the soft skill encouragement at General Assembly, such as peer/mob programming and rotating group mentorship. I’ve taken this not as a lesson, but as a belief: when a group asks for help, offers advice, and researches with a purpose, any "I don’t know” turns from a feeling to a call-to-action ending with “…yet!”

I’m excited to continue building on the engineering skills I’ve gained at General Assembly to combine with my solutions focused outlook and approach. With all that said, the SEI program is not a finish line, but a strong start to a lifetime of continued learning, pushing the envelope, and challenging myself. I’m looking forward to this marathon, and am excited to continue researching, collaborating, and “tinkering” to find more solutions.
